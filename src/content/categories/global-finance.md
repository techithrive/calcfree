  Tax and finance rules don't travel, which is exactly why generic calculators fall short outside the UK. This section covers specific situations in Canada and Australia where the local detail changes the answer. Buying in Toronto, for instance, means paying both Ontario's provincial land transfer tax and the city's own municipal one — something national tools routinely miss. In Australia, HECS-HELP repayments step up through income thresholds that shift each year, and a novated lease only makes sense once you understand the residual at the end.

  The calculators here are built around those particulars and cite the relevant authority — the Ontario Ministry of Finance, the City of Toronto, or the ATO — so the figures can be checked. Thresholds and rates are held in editable data and flagged for annual review. They're planning estimates in the local currency, designed to give residents a realistic starting point rather than a number built for a different country's rules.
