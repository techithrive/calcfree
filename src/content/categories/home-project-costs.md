intro_copy: |
  Planning a project around the house usually starts with one question: what's this going to cost? These calculators give you a grounded estimate before you ring a contractor or load up a trolley at the builders' merchant. Rather than a single vague "per square foot" figure, they break the job into the materials and quantities you'll actually need — posts, panels, blocks, base gravel, pavers — and then add a realistic labour range.

  Material prices vary a lot by region, supplier and time of year, so the figures are editable defaults you can adjust to local quotes, and every tool says plainly that it's a planning estimate. The section also covers the running-cost decisions that come with a home: whether solar panels pay back in a reasonable time, and how an electric car compares with petrol once you factor in your own electricity tariff. Useful numbers to argue with a quote, not a fixed price.
