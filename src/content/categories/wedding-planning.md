  Wedding costs are wildly variable and weirdly hard to pin down, partly because so much depends on where you are and how many people you invite. This section approaches it honestly: a region-aware estimate that reflects what couples in different parts of the UK actually spend, broken down by the categories that eat the budget — venue and catering, photography, attire, flowers and the rest.

  Guest count is the single biggest lever, so the tool puts it front and centre, and it shows a category breakdown rather than one intimidating total, so you can see where the money goes and where you might trim. It's deliberately free of judgement about budget size; a modest wedding and a lavish one are both valid, and the figures simply reflect typical spending you can adjust. Think of it as a sensible starting framework for a conversation about priorities, not a price list.
